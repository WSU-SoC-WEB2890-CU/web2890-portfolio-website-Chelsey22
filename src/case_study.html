Case Study

Description Project Name: Fear the Paranormal

Overview:

Fear the Paranormal is a website I designed and built to explore and share the world of paranormal experiences. The site serves as a digital hub for stories, theories, and multimedia content focused on the eerie and unexplained—from ghost sightings to urban legends.

Goals:

The goal of this project was to create a visually immersive, mobile-friendly website that engages visitors through storytelling and design. I wanted to practice responsive web design, improve my layout and CSS skills, and bring a creative concept to life. Process: I began with wireframes and mood boards to establish the look and feel—dark tones, bold typography, and eerie imagery. I used HTML, SASS (with some Bootstrap for layout), and a bit of JavaScript for interactivity. Accessibility and mobile responsiveness were important throughout the build.

Challenges:

One challenge was making sure images and text balanced well on mobile screens without sacrificing visual impact. Another was fine tuning hover effects and background layers to keep the design spooky but still user friendly. I refined styles using media queries and adjusted layout elements with flexbox and grid.

Outcome:

The final site feels immersive, informative, and visually aligned with the theme. I learned a lot about balancing creative design with technical execution. In the future, I'd love to expand the site to include a user submitted stories section and connect the products section to a database.
